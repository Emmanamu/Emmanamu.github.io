Newsletter End of First Term 2019

Keyworkers have been making appointments for discussions with you all individually around how well the children have settled and what activities they are interested and involved in. Learning priorities have been identified and the “next steps” in children’s learning agreed together. Next steps are a good way to show specific progress that the children make within a specific area of their learning. Although we know it is impossible to set next steps in all aspects of children’s learning we can track a “learning journey” for some aspects that seem most relevant and important. Children now have their ‘Records of Achievement’. We aim to involve the children in thinking about their own learning and how it is successful for them. Through this our intention is to strengthen children’s understanding of how they learn best and support them in their confidence and enthusiasm as independent learners. Please continue to share with your keyworker any highlights in the children’s progress and achievements and add to their folder. Governors The Purley Partnership Federation Governing body is made up of members of the local community including parents from our school and Christ Church School. Governors are integral to the work we do. They ensure that the quality of the school is maintained and lead on our vision in line with our aims and ethos. Look out for opportunities to meet with Governors in the New Year. The Chair of Governors is : David Colpus The vice-Chair is Paula Reed and she can be contacted via the School Office.
